Miami's Main Character

The bold, the busy, and the beautifully chaotic trends Alix Earle has brought to life. See what trends this TikTok star is starting next. What began with GRWM videos and off-the-cuff outfit reveals has turned into a full-fledged aesthetic shift. Alix doesn’t just follow trends — she sets the tempo, often stepping into the spotlight wearing layered textures, clashing colors, and enough personality to make any minimalist blush. Her approach is equal parts impulsive and intentional, echoing the spirit of a generation that’s tired of clean lines and curated neutrals.

If minimalism had its whisper era, 2025 is full volume. This year, subtle solids are taking a back seat while patterns—bold, bright, and often battling for attention—are running the show. From checkerboard bikinis to neon florals layered over leopard print, fashion has embraced a sensory overload. Alix’s influence can be seen in the return of low-rise prints, sparkly accessories with everything, and the unapologetic mixing of eras and aesthetics. One scroll through her page and it’s clear: maximalism is back, and it’s louder than ever.

This visual noise is more than just a styling choice — it’s a shift in mood. After years of beige, taupe, and Instagram-perfect minimalism, there's a desire to be seen, felt, and remembered. Young creatives are leaning into fashion that’s expressive, not polished; impulsive, not edited. Alix’s personal brand mirrors this perfectly: a bit chaotic, undeniably confident, and impossible to ignore. Her outfit choices may look spur-of-the-moment, but they resonate with the energy of someone who knows exactly who she is — and isn’t afraid to let the world know.

Beyond fashion, this new maximalist wave is spilling into beauty, interiors, and even branding. Think glitter liner in the middle of the day, checkerboard nails, and multicolored throw pillows that don’t match anything but still feel right. Brands are taking note, leaning into high-contrast visuals and design that pops off the shelf. Alix, knowingly or not, has helped reframe chaos as charm — and in doing so, made the idea of “too much” feel just right.

As we move through the year, expect to see more trends rooted in instinct over polish. Confidence is the new curation, and the line between “put together” and “thrown together” is delightfully blurry. Alix Earle isn’t just wearing the trends — she’s embodying the mood of a cultural pivot. One that embraces boldness, self-expression, and a little bit of beautiful mess.
